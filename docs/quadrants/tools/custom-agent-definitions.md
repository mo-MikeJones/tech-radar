## Summary
Custom instructions, prompt files and agent configuration that tailor AI coding assistants to specific team workflows, coding standards and business context.

## Why we're interested
Out-of-the-box AI assistants are generic. Custom agent definitions allow teams to encode their standards, patterns and domain knowledge directly into the AI.

## Current status
Adopted by several teams. Shared prompt libraries and custom instructions are becoming standard practice. Guidelines published on the developer portal.

## Evidence & feedback
Teams with well-crafted custom instructions report significantly higher quality AI outputs. Shared libraries reduce duplication and accelerate onboarding of new engineers.

## Known risks / concerns
Poorly written instructions can degrade AI output quality. Custom configurations require maintenance as tooling and codebases evolve.

## Recommended audience
Engineering leads and experienced developers defining team AI workflows.

## Next steps
Establish a shared repository for reusable agent definitions. Define versioning and contribution guidelines for the shared library.
