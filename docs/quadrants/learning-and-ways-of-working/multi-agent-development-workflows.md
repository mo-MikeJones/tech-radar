## Summary
Workflows in which multiple specialised AI agents collaborate to complete complex development tasks — for example, a planner agent, a coder agent and a reviewer agent working in sequence.

## Why we're interested
Single-agent approaches hit limits on complex, multi-step tasks. Multi-agent architectures show promise in handling tasks that span planning, implementation and validation.

## Current status
Under active exploration. Proof-of-concept workflows built and demonstrated. Not yet ready for production use.

## Evidence & feedback
Early experiments show multi-agent workflows can automate full feature development cycles. Coordination overhead and error propagation between agents are active challenges.

## Known risks / concerns
Significantly higher potential for compounding errors. Output quality is highly sensitive to prompt design and agent orchestration. Requires strong human oversight.

## Recommended audience
AI-forward engineering teams willing to invest in structured experimentation. Frontier team members exploring the limits of agentic AI.

## Next steps
Run structured experiments on two or three well-defined use cases. Document findings and share back to the engineering community.
