## Summary
Exploration of how engineering team structures, roles and ceremonies can be adapted to maximise the benefit of AI tooling, including AI-augmented planning, review and knowledge management.

## Why we're interested
AI tooling has the potential to change how teams are sized, structured and how work flows. Understanding these changes early enables proactive adaptation rather than reactive adjustment.

## Current status
Under active exploration. Research phase. No formal recommendations yet.

## Evidence & feedback
Early signals that AI tooling can increase effective output per engineer. Teams experimenting with smaller, more autonomous units supported by AI tooling show promising early results.

## Known risks / concerns
Structural changes based on AI productivity assumptions carry significant risk if assumptions prove incorrect. Changes must be made carefully with strong measurement in place.

## Recommended audience
Engineering leaders and organisational design practitioners interested in the future of engineering organisations.

## Next steps
Complete research phase. Publish findings. Pilot one or two structural experiments with willing teams and strong measurement.

CTO Office / Engineering Leadership
