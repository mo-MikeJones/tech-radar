## Summary
Model Context Protocol (MCP) is an open standard for connecting AI models to external tools, data sources and services, enabling AI agents to interact beyond their training data.

## Why we're interested
MCP is rapidly becoming the standard interface for AI-tool integration. Early adoption positions us well as the ecosystem matures around this open standard.

## Current status
Under evaluation. MCP servers being built for internal tools. Integration patterns being defined.

## Evidence & feedback
Strong industry adoption behind MCP. Internal experiments show good results for connecting AI to internal APIs and data sources. Ecosystem tooling still maturing.

## Known risks / concerns
Standard is still evolving. MCP servers can expose sensitive internal systems to AI agents — careful access control and audit logging are essential.

## Recommended audience
Platform engineers building AI integrations. AI product teams designing agent capabilities.

## Next steps
Publish internal MCP integration guidelines. Build MCP servers for the two or three highest-value internal tool integrations.

AI Platform Team
