## Summary
Patterns and protocols for enabling AI agents to communicate and collaborate with each other, including the emerging Agent-to-Agent (A2A) protocol and MCP-based coordination.

## Why we're interested
Complex tasks increasingly require multiple specialised agents working together. Standardised agent communication protocols reduce integration complexity and improve interoperability.

## Current status
Very early exploration. Monitoring the evolving A2A protocol standard. No production implementations yet.

## Evidence & feedback
Strong industry interest in agent-to-agent communication standards. Early protocol implementations are experimental. Practical benefits depend on multi-agent architecture becoming more common in our workflows.

## Known risks / concerns
Standards are immature and evolving quickly. Early adoption risks incompatibility as the standard matures. Complex failure modes in multi-agent systems require careful design.

## Recommended audience
AI platform engineers and architects designing multi-agent systems. Frontier team members exploring advanced agentic architectures.

## Next steps
Continue monitoring A2A protocol development. Run a small experiment connecting two internal agents via MCP coordination. Share findings with the platform team.

AI Platform Team
