What is the AI4Dev Radar?

The AI4Dev Radar provides a transparent view of the tools, practices, platforms and ways of working that are being explored, trialled and adopted across the Met Office engineering community.

Each item is assigned to a lifecycle phase based on its current level of organisational understanding, evidence, adoption and support. The purpose of the radar is not to mandate technology choices, but to help teams understand what is being investigated, what is being proven in delivery, what is available for wider use, and what is no longer being pursued.

We use five lifecycle phases based on the GDS delivery model:

  • DISCOVER — Items we are actively exploring to understand potential value, use cases, costs, risks and organisational fit.
  • ALPHA — Items undergoing initial experimentation and proof of concept activity, evaluated by a small number of teams.
  • BETA — Items that have demonstrated value in real-world delivery and are being trialled more widely.
  • LIVE — Items proven in practice, supported for wider use across the organisation.
  • RETIRED — Items that are no longer being actively pursued or recommended.

The radar supports transparency, learning and collaboration across the engineering community. By making the status, evidence and direction of travel visible, we can reduce duplication of effort and help teams make informed decisions about where to invest their time and energy.

What is the purpose?

The AI4Dev Radar provides a transparent view of the tools, practices, platforms and ways of working being explored across the Met Office engineering community. Its purpose is to help teams make informed decisions, learn from one another, and understand where the organisation is investing effort in agentic development and AI-enabled delivery.

The radar is not a list of approved technologies, nor is it intended to mandate technology choices. Instead, it provides visibility of what is being investigated, what is being trialled, what has demonstrated value in delivery, and what is now considered part of the standard engineering toolkit.

By bringing this information together in one place, the radar helps to:

  • Share lessons learned and practical experiences across teams.
  • Reduce duplication of effort and repeated experimentation.
  • Provide visibility of emerging tools, platforms and practices.
  • Support evidence-based technology and process decisions.
  • Create a common understanding of where the organisation is on its AI4Dev journey.
  • Turn isolated pockets of success into reusable organisational capability.

How do we maintain it?

The AI4Dev Radar is maintained by the AI4Dev community and Evaluation Group on behalf of the wider engineering organisation.

New entries can be proposed by any engineer, team or community member. Items move through lifecycle phases based on evidence gathered from experimentation and real-world usage:

Discover → Alpha → Beta → Live → Retired

The radar depends on active participation from the engineering community. Teams are encouraged to share successes, challenges, lessons learned and recommendations so that knowledge can be reused across the organisation rather than remaining within individual projects or teams.
